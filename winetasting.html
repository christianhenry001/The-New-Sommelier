Step 1:

Tilt your glass of wine and hold it over a white background under neutral light. You will be looking for 3 things, the hue, intensity of color, and the viscosity. Each of these observations will tell you something different about the wine.

Step 2:

Smell the wine and attempt to create a profile of its aromas. Try to spot several fruit flavors and well as several herbal flavors. Look for any other flavors as well such as leather, graphite, oakiness, etc.

Step 3:

Take a large enough sip that you can pass the wine across your whole palate before swallowing. Observe the structure of the wine such as the tannins or acidity. Identify the flavors that you couldn't smell before.

Step 4:

Make an assessment of the qualities of your wine. Write down the tasting notes. Additionally, you can rate this wine and/or compare it to other wines you've tried.
